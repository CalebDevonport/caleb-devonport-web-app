Reading List 2021

I'm proud to say I read a whole lot last year. I challenged myself to read 30 pages a day for the whole year and I managed it, reading 29 books in the process!

I used to read loads as a kid, but mostly stopped as I got older. I still enjoyed reading when I got a new book, but normally I would just binge it in a week or so, then not touch another one for months at a time. A couple of things happened last year to reignite my love of reading. Coincidentally, both of these things happened on the 4th of January.

Firstly, Boris Johnson announced another coronavirus lockdown. While I didn't know how long it would be at the time (it ended up being like 3 months), I knew I'd be stuck inside for a good long while, and so I was looking for a long term goal that I could work towards.
Secondly, I saw this reddit post, with someone showing off how much they had read in 2020. The post really impressed me - there was such a varied selection of books, and the stack was so tall! I was inspired - I knew I was looking for something to do during lockdown, I knew I loved reading and wanted to do it more, and I now knew I wanted to take a similar picture of myself with a big stack of books.

And so I set myself the challenge of reading as much as I could in the year. I wanted to create a positive habit of reading regularly, as I knew that if I didn't commit to it I would just slip back into my normal ways of finishing a book then not touching another for ages. I'm quite goal oriented, so I've always found I have more success with stuff if I set some form of challenge. I decided I would try to read 30 pages a day - by no means a huge amount, but enough that if I didn't read for a few days I would quickly fall behind. I read every evening before bed, which was a much better habit than my previous bedtime routine of scrolling endlessly through social media.

I was very glad I decided to do this, as during lockdown reading was a huge help in keeping me sane. I started getting up earlier and reading a little in the mornings before work as well; it helped me to relax in the mornings and prepare for the workday rather than waking up shortly before I was meant to start and sprinting downstairs to my pc in a panic.
Eventually lockdown ended, and with more things going on I did find it harder to keep up the habit, but I kept to my goal. To ensure I was keeping to my 30 pages a day, I decided to create a spreadsheet, tracking the dates I'd finished different books and making sure I was keeping up the pace. I got a bit obsessed with the stats, and created a few charts as well to see which authors I'd been reading the most. Anyone interested can find it here.

And so here we are in 2022. I'm proud to say I managed what I'd set out to do - I had a wonderful time reading a variety of books, and I made reading a regular habit that I aim to continue going forwards. Pictured below is me holding all the books I read last year.

I don't think I could pick a single favourite out of the bunch. To me, reading is a way of immersing myself in another world, and so I think I enjoyed the books where I lost myself the most. To that end, "The Stand" and "The Lord of the Rings" were probably my favourites - the length of both epics really gives you time to connect with the characters, and I'm unashamed to say both brought me to tears. I thoroughly enjoyed revisiting the Harry Potter series again too. I think, like many others, Harry Potter was what sparked my love of reading (I read the whole series at least 5 times as a kid), and re-reading them again after a decade or so was a real treat.
I didn't dislike any of the books, but there were definitely some I wasn't as keen on. I didn't think "The Shining" was amazing, and I wasn't blown away by "Murder on the Orient Express", but given both are hugely popular classics maybe my expectations were just too high. I re-read the whole "Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy" series as well, and while they were great at some points and often had me laughing, I remember enjoying them a lot more when I read them the first time around.

Anyway, to anyone reading this, thanks a lot, and I hope you enjoyed. Maybe this will inspire you to start reading some more, or maybe you already are!
