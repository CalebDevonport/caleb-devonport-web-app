Reading List 2021

I'm proud to say I read a whole lot last year. I set myself the target of reading 30 pages a day for the whole year and I managed it!

I used to read loads as a kid, then stopped as I got older. I still enjoyed reading when I got a new book, but normally I would just binge through a book in a week or so, then not touch another one for months at a time. A couple of things happened last year to reignite my love of reading.

Both these things happened on the 4th of January - firstly, Boris Johnson announced another coronavirus related lockdown. While I didn't know how long it would be at the time (it ended up being like 3 months), I knew I'd be stuck inside for a good long while, and so I was looking for something that I could work towards over this time.
Secondly, I saw this reddit post, with someone showing off how much they had read in 2020. I was impressed, and inspired - if they could do it why couldn't I?

And so I set myself a challenge of reading as much as I could in the year. Shortly after this goal was refined to reading what I believed to be a sustainable level of 30 pages per day. I ended up an average of 33.9 pages per day, with a total of 12382 pages, and 29 books.
